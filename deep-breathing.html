Deep Breathing

Play in Browser Watch Gameplay Source Code

Game Jams are always a great way to test your stamina, determination, and discipline. It also allows me to reflect my growth as a game developer year over year.


This project was made in 72 hours for the Ludum Dare 48 Game Jam. The team consisted of myself doing 3D modeling, animation, and visual scripting, and a fellow programmer handling systems, events, and mechanics. The theme was “Deeper and Deeper”. Below I will outline each step of the development process for a game jam.

Breathing Mechanic

We decided to focus on creating a mechanic for breathing in and out and a steady pulse, and the character transitioning into deeper states of concentration. The idea is to breathe away distractions that take you out of the moment. Our result was very simple, you hold the space bar to breathe in and release to let out your breath. A puff of air is then shot in the direction that the player specifies, and dissolves any distractions in its path.

Modelling

The main obstacle to deep concentration are distractions, so I modelled a bunch of things that take up space in one’s mind. During our brainstorm we came up with various thoughts, and randomly generate text on a thought bubble icon. We also included worries of time, money, and deadlines. We added material objects that can deter one from being present, such as phones and TVs. There are also environmental distractions, like external sounds and a storm.

Transitions

We knew we needed the environment of the game to be dynamic to push the idea of deeper states of concentration. So we made 3 transitions into different backdrops to represent the deepening of concentration. The player goes from their living room to space to an immaterial geometric plane. Finally, the player arrives at Nirvana, a white void of peace. At this stage, the player and all distractions vanish.
