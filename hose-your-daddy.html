Hose Your Daddy

Play in Browser Watch Gameplay Source Code

This game was made for the 2020 Game Maker’s Tool Kit game jam. A game jam is a marathon where developers must create a game in 48 hours. It is a wonderful exercise of being able to work efficiently in a restricted time frame. It tests a team’s ability to time manage effectively, prioritizing tasks and being able to make sacrifices for their vision. I was able to collaborate with an amazing team of artists, programmers, and designers for this event, and at the end of a weekend we were able to do more than I had done with any game project.

The theme for the game jam was “Out of Control”. Our team spend the first few hours ideating on these words, and came up with the concept of a chaotic hose that sprays water in random directions. The result was a goofy and wonderful platform brawler, where you use hoses to push your opponent off the stage. This game took a lot of inspiration from the Super Smash Brothers series, but gave it our unique flavor.

Droplet Particles

I implemented an emission system to spray at your opponents. The hose instatiates a droplet at the position of the nozzle at a given time interval. We had to account for each player's droplets now affecting themselves, only the opponent, so we separated the two player's particles into different physics layers that do not collide with their corresponding owner.

Hose Physics

I wanted to make the hose react to movement, and made the rope as a Linked List of lines that react to the next and previous segments in the chain. The first segment is fixed to a specified position, and the last segment is attached to the transform of the player. By looping through the Linked List, the segments respond according to the movement of the other segments, giving the hose a semi-realistic, physics driven rendering.
